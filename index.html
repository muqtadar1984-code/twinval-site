TwinVal ingests live readings from building-grade IoT sensors — structural, environmental, electrical, utility. Each stream is conditioned using a Savitzky–Golay filter and z-score normalisation before entering the digital twin.

The twin computes five technical indicators — SHF, ESF, USS, PDP, CI — against thresholds independently certified by licensed engineers. These resolve into a Real-Time Property Market Value (RTPMV) anchored to the government valuation baseline.

Every RTPMV cycle is hashed and linked to the previous cycle — a continuous chain of cryptographic proof that no later party can alter without detection.

Five technical indicators. One tamper-evident valuation. Continuously updated from live sensor data. Threshold parameters set by certified engineers — never by an algorithm.

Technical Indicators

Five measures. One composite health factor.

SHF
Structural Health Factor

Vibration, strain, and load stress — the physical integrity of the asset.

ESF
Environmental Sustainability Factor

Energy efficiency, emissions profile, and environmental compliance.

USS
Utility & Service Score

HVAC, electrical, and plumbing service reliability across the property.

PDP
Physical Deterioration Proxy

Ageing and wear measured against asset life expectancy.

CI
Confidence Index

Market and data confidence weighting — composite signal quality.

RTPMV         = Land Value + (Structure Value × Health Factor)
Health Factor = SHF × ESF × (1 − USS) × PDP × CI

Thresholds independently certified by licensed structural and environmental engineers against ASHRAE 90.1, ASCE 7-22, and BS EN 1990 Eurocode standards.
